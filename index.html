#MeToo - 2017, New York City, NY

When I first moved to college, the #MeToo Movement had gone from just a Women's March protest to a full-fledged investigation of Hollywood and its treatment of female-bodied industry workers. Ironically, I was in a Contemporary Feminisms class the morning of the Harvey Weinstein case, and we discussed the autonomy of female bodies as it pertains to something like sexual assault. I went home that night and spoke to my roommate about it, who then told me their #MeToo story. We decided to work together and create a series of images depicting the dissonance sexual assault can bring to a person. Their name is Eliot Sandbach.

Voices 4 Chechnya Rally - October 2017, New York City, NY

Voices4 is a non-profit (FC) LGBTQ+ organization based in New York City, on 8th ave (FC). In 2017, they had a march from Stonewall to Trump Tower in protest of the decision to throw LGBTQ+ identifying people into what seemed like concentration camps, most likely inducing gay conversion therapy, in Chechnya, Russia. Captured below are some moments from the march.

National Geographic Expedition - July 2017, Prague and Bohemia, Czech Republic

When I graduated high school, I had the opportunity to go with National Geographic on one of their student expedition photographers to the CZech Republic. I spent 12 days immersing myself in culture and history, as well as working with four established photographers: Ami Vitale, Whitney Tressel, Alex Milan Tracy, and Sarah-Marie Land.
