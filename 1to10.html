HOW WE HEAL

The following is a photo essay depciting the bodies and faces of those who live with four different types of autoimmune illnesses: Celiac Disease and PCOS, eczema, ulcerative colitis, and Chron's Disease. The purpose of these photographs is not to make the illness the forefront; but rather how these individuals heal themselves from a pain that is constant. There has been a push in recent years to romanticize and aestheticize self-care; the now-famous store Lush being one example of how self-healing practices are now supposed to be viewed as beautiful, even luxurious. But what about when self care is not beautiful? What about when self care means taking seven different types of medication, or missing out on outings with friends, or throwing up until you feel better after sticking a needle in your leg? For those who live with chronic pain, self-care means something different. The following images depict the various methods and avenues people with chronic pain decide to heal themselves. The purpose of these images is to not show these women as weak, but rather to call attention to the strength that comes from knowing how to take care of their bodies in the way that they need. According to the American Autoimmune and Related Diseases Association, around 20% of all Americans have an Autoimmune Disease. Of that number, 75% are female-bodied people. This is a way to conceptualize just a few of the women who live within that 75%
