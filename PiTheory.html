From One To Ten

The Space Between Pain and Recovery

A Transmedia Project












The purpose of this project is to visualize what it is like to live with autoimmune and/or chronic pain. This pain is invisible yet is still classified as “physical”; those who live with it must reconcile themselves within the liminal space between pain and recovery. A space that, by definition, is transformation, and yet the nature of the pain prevents it from moving. There is growth, but it is not necessarily linear. It is the purest form of ironic paradox: those who live with chronic pain are constantly working towards the goal of “recovery”, as all people who are injured are. But the reality of a pain that never truly goes away means we are endlessly chasing a solution that doesn’t exist, at least not for us. We are in a space of transformation, but we are stuck in that we are always transforming, yet never recover. We are Sisyphus, pushing our primordial flaws up the mountain, never seeing rest. *TK Something About Pi*
